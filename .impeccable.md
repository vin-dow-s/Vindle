# Vindle — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Casual word-game players, French- and English-speaking, all ages. Two contexts:
1. **The daily ritual** — a quick, cozy "Mot du jour" with a clever definition, played once a day with morning-coffee energy.
2. **Light competition** — climbing a leaderboard against friends and strangers.
Mobile-first (played with thumbs), but must feel great on desktop too.

### Brand Personality
Charming, warm, a little handcrafted — NOT a sterile productivity tool. Three words:
**playful · cozy · characterful.** The signature is the hand-script "Vindle" wordmark
(Alex Brush) floating over a soft lavender world. It should feel like a beloved indie
game, not a corporate app. Emotional goal: a small daily spark of delight and satisfaction.

### Aesthetic Direction
Soft / pastel, refined-playful. **Light theme only** — the lavender identity IS the brand;
dark mode is not the point.

Reference to PRESERVE faithfully (the original Vindle):
- lavender field `#dcd0ff`, white rounded card, Alex Brush cursive wordmark, Poppins body
- `#008000` green "correct" / `#8a8686` gray "absent" / `#d2afff` purple tile borders
- the falling **purple-hearts** win celebration (heart.png)

Elevate with:
- intentional spatial rhythm and a refined OKLCH palette tinted toward the lavender hue
- real **Phosphor icons** (NEVER emojis), weighted to sit with Poppins
- purposeful **motion**: one orchestrated page-load stagger, Wordle tile-flip reveals,
  key-press feedback, hover micro-interactions, the heart-rain celebration

Anti-references: generic Tailwind gray dashboards, glassmorphism, neon-on-dark "AI" look,
gradient text, an icon-with-rounded-corners above every heading.

### Design Principles
1. **Preserve the soul.** Lavender + cursive wordmark + heart-rain are sacred; everything
   new must feel native to that world.
2. **Motion with intent.** Animate state changes (reveals, feedback, celebration), never
   decoration. Always honor `prefers-reduced-motion`.
3. **Icons, never emoji.** Phosphor only, sized/weighted to match the type.
4. **Tactile play.** Keys, tiles and buttons respond instantly and physically (press, lift, flip).
5. **Calm hierarchy.** Generous breathing room; few type sizes with strong contrast; the
   purple accent used sparingly so it stays special.
